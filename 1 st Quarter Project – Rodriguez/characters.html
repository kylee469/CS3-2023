Characters

Charlie Spring/Joe Locke

Charlie Spring, portrayed by Joe Locke, is a 15 year old British teen, and he is Gay. He is Nick's boyfriend and Tori's little brother. Charlie Spring is a kind-hearted, thoughtful, and artistic young man. He's known for his caring nature and his warm personality. He is openly gay and has a deep passion for drumming. His character suffered from bullying which made him tend to worry and overthink things.

Nick Nelson/Kit Connor

Nick Nelson, portrayed by Kit Connor, is a 16 year old British/French teen, just a year above Charlie, and he is Bi. He is Charlie's boyfriend and he has a dog named Nellie. Due to both his appearance and his participation in rugby, Nick was initially stereotyped as nothing more than a rude, abrasive, and empty-minded rugby lad. But in reality, he is a warm, considerate, and protective character just like Charlie.

Tara Jones/Corinna Brown

Tara Jones, portrayed by Corinna Brown, is a 16 year old British teen and she is a Lesbian. She is Darcy's girlfriend and Nick's childhood friend. Tara is shown to be straight-laced, quiet and observant. She constantly keeps up with her girlfriend's loud and energetic personality.

Darcy Olsson/Kizzy Edgell

Darcy Olsson, portrayed by Kizzy Edgell, is a 16 year old British teen and is a Lesbian. She is Tara's girlfriend and she knows how to play the trumpet. Darcy is extremely chaotic and extroverted, loving to make the people around her laugh, especially Tara. One thing about her is she absolutely despises dresses.

Elle Argent/Yasmin Finney

Elle Argent, portrayed by Yasmin Finney, is a 16 year old multi-ethnic teen and is a Transgender. She is Tao's girlfriend and Charlie's bestfriend. She is in the same class as Darcy and Tara. Elle is overall very sweet, caring, and friendly. She is also very creative and artistic which explains why she went into art school.

Tao Xu/William Gao

Tao Xu, portrayed by William Gao, is a 15 year old Chinese teen, he is the only straight guy in his group. He is Elle's girlfriend and Charlie's bestest friend. Tao is initially very distrustful of Nick, believing his flirtatious attitude towards Charlie to be a ruse. But seeing how much Nick brings happiness to Charlie, Tao backs down. He is a very loud and cheerful character and is quite insensitive but he sure is a protective friend.

Isaac Henderson/Tobie Donovan

Isaac Henderson, portrayed by Tobie Donovan, is a 15 year old British teen, he is Aromantic and Asexual. He is Charlie's bestest friend. He initially wasn't a part of the novel but was then added to the series. He is an extreme bookworm so he brings a book around all the time, he is very quiet but observant.

Tori Spring/Jenny Walser

Tori Spring, is a 16 year old British teen, she is Aromantic and Asexual. She is Charlie's older sister. She has her own book, Solitaire, by the same author. Tori is an introverted, very cynical, and pessimistic teenager.

Nellie Nelson/Echo

Nellie Nelson, portrayed by Echo, is Nick Nelson's dog. Although she is quite old, Nellie is very cheerful and energetic.

Imogen Heaney/Rhea Norwood

Imogen, portrayed by Rhea Norwood, is a 16 year old British teen. She is one of Nick's closest friend. Imogen is sweet, bubbly, and extroverted. Imogen is somewhat a hopeless romantic, pining for Nick's affection despite being visibly uncomfortable. She used to be an annoying character but soon had a character development

Ben Hope/Sebastian Croft

Ben Hope, portrayed by Sebastian Croft, is a 16 year old British teen. He is one of the main antagonist in the series. He used to date Charlie and Imogen but he was a huge red flag. Ben is portrayed as snarky, cunning, manipulative, and violent, with his nature stemming from being a closeted bisexual.

Sarah Nelson/Olivia Colman

Sarah Nelson, portrayed by Olivia Colman, is Nick's mom. At some point, she divorced Nick's dad, after which she raised Nick and his brother, David Nelson, mostly by herself. Sarah is a warm and understanding mother to Nick, and immediately supported him when he came out to her. He often comes to her for advice.
